Summer Bucket List ☑️

The summer bucket list format is everywhere this week — creators tick off everything from beach days to frozen yogurt runs. Slipping the dental checkup onto the list makes it feel like part of the adventure, not a chore. Parents love sharing this with their own kids.

What the trend is

Show a summer bucket list — on paper, whiteboard, or text cards — and tick off activities one by one on camera. Dead simple, massively shareable, because every parent relates to planning summer with their kids.
Your version: Dr. Aaron or a team member reads off a short list: "First ice cream... ✅ Pool day... ✅ Kids dental checkup... [big smile] ✅!" End with: "799 York Mills — summer's sorted 🦷☀️"

Dracula Smile Transform 🧛

Tame Impala × JENNIE's "Dracula" remix has 545,000+ TikTok videos right now — it's THE transformation format of summer 2026. You enter dramatically in dark mode, the beat drops, and something transforms. For a dental office: dark plaque → bright clean smile. The irony of a vampire at the dentist is pure comedy gold.

What the trend is

Two phases: walk in under dim moody lighting (dark scrubs, low light, dramatic face). The JENNIE beat hits, flash-cut. Then bright warm light reveals something transformed. That's it — the whole format is the contrast.
Your version: Dr. Noah or Dr. Aaron walks in under moody light. Flash-cut on the beat drop. Then bright lights → kid in the chair beaming with thumbs up. Text overlay: "From dark (plaque 🦇) to bright ✨ — that's what we do." The vampire-at-the-dentist angle is comedy gold.
Caption: Dracula teeth? Not on our watch 🧛→✨ #torontokidsdental
Hashtags: #draculatrend #dracula #jennie #kidsdentist #torontokidsdental
Sound: "Dracula (JENNIE Remix)" — Tame Impala × JENNIE. Use the 0:28–0:38 dramatic drop for the flash-cut reveal moment.

POV: Zero Cavities 🎉

The POV format never goes away because it's pure storytelling. "POV: the moment we tell a kid they have zero cavities" — the dentist's face lights up, the team cheers, maybe they earn a coin for the prize shelf. Parents of kids about to start the dentist journey share this every single time.

What the trend is

Add "POV:" as a text overlay and show a first-person moment from the dentist's perspective. The camera becomes the dentist — or the proud parent watching. The more genuine the reaction, the more shares.
Your version: Dr. Gideon or Dr. Noah says "ZERO cavities this summer!" on camera, then cut to the kid's face — pure joy. Quick cut to them cashing in their coins for a prize. Keep it warm and real — no performance needed.
Caption: POV: zero cavities 🎉🦷 this is our FAVOURITE moment #torontokidsdental
Hashtags: #zerocavities #nocavities #kidsdentist #pov #torontokidsdental
Sound: "Life Feels So Good" — napsea. Use the warm 0:06–0:16 opening — it's the perfect feel-good backdrop for the big reveal.

😂 Skit⚡ Trending comedy format★ 8

Your Dentist Knows 🕵️

The "Your dentist knows EXACTLY what you're doing" skit format is blowing up — dentists pointing at the camera and revealing the funny truth they can tell from one look inside a mouth. For a kids' office it becomes adorably relatable: "we know you skipped brushing last night." Parents and kids are both howling.

What the trend is

Point slowly at the camera. Text cards reveal hilarious-but-true things the dentist can tell just from looking. Always ends warmly — the comedy comes from recognition, never judgment.
Your version: Dr. Aaron or Dr. Noah points at camera: "Your dentist knows… 🕵️" → text cards: "…you had gummies last night." / "…this is a brand-new toothbrush (the old one was never used)." / "…you brushed for about 10 seconds." Final card: "We love you anyway. We're here to help 🦷❤️"
Caption: your dentist knows 😏🕵️ we see everything — and we still love you #torontokidsdental
Hashtags: #yourdentistknows #dentisthumor #kidsdentist #torontokidsdental
Sound: "What's Up?" — 4 Non Blondes. Use the 1:09–1:19 iconic "hey hey hey hey!" singalong — perfect dramatic energy for the pointing reveal.

Sunday Checkup Vlog 📱

Authentic, handheld vlog content is thriving right now — people trust creators who film it like a text to a friend, not a polished ad. A quick Sunday office vlog from one of your dentists feels warm, real, and human. Parents book appointments after seeing the actual team, not a stock photo.

What the trend is

Hold the phone like you're texting a friend — casual, handheld, no tripod. Narrate your real Sunday morning: "it's [name] at Toronto Kids Dental, here's what we're up to." Show the real people, the real space, the real energy. No script, no perfection.
Your version: Dr. Aaron or Dr. Noah does a 30-second selfie vlog: quick wave, pan the office (chairs look spotless, tools ready), wave at a team member, then: "Summer checkup slots going fast — come see us at 799 York Mills 🦷" Close camera.
Caption: sunday vlog @ toronto kids dental 📱🦷 summer slots filling fast — link in bio! #torontokidsdental
Hashtags: #sundayvlog #kidsdentist #behindthescenes #torontokidsdental
Sound: "Wings (Sped Up Nightcore)" — Birdy. Use the 0:40–0:50 cinematic swell for the closing shot — adds a beautiful lift that turns a simple vlog into a moment.
